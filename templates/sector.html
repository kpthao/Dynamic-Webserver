If not already noticed there are many kinds of renewable energy. There are solar, wind, wood, geothermal, hydropower, bioenergy and more! At least annually renewable resource consumption begain being tracked during the late 1940s and early 1950s and spans all the way to the last couple of years. One thing to note is the measurement of Trillion (BTU). BTU is the abbreviation that stands for the British Thermal Unit and is used to measure sources of energy.

Solar Field

Solar Energy

  • Most abundant of all enery resources
  • Can even be harnessed in cloudy weather.
  • Rate at which solar energy is intercepted by the Earth is about 10,000 times greater than the rate at which human kind consumes energy.
  • Solar technologies can deliver the following:
    1. Heat
    2. Cooling
    3. Natural Lighting
    4. Fuels for host of applications
  • Solar Energies convert sunlight into electrical energy either through photovoltaic panels or through mirrors that concentrate solar radiation.

Cited from United Nations Climate Action

Wind Energy

  • Wind energy harnesses the kinetic energy of moving air by using large wind turbines
  • Many parts of the world have strong wind speeds, but the best locations for generating wind power are sometimes remote areas. Offshore wind power offers much potential.
  • Over time wind turbines developed into larger structures with bigger rotor diameters

Cited from United Nations Climate Action

Hydropower Energy

  • Hydropower harnesses the energy of water moving from higher to lower elevations
  • It can be genereated from either reserviors and rivers
    1. Reserviors: these kinds of power plants rely on stored water
    2. Run-Of-River: these kinds of power plants harness energy from the available flow of the river
  • Hydropower reservoirs often have multiple uses - providing drinking water, water for irrigation, flood and drought control, navigation services, as well as energy supply.
  • Hydro Energy can be negatively impacted by climate-induced droughts or changes to ecosystems which impact rainfall patters.

Cited from United Nations Climate Action

Bio Energy

  • Bioenergy is produced from a variety of organic materials, called biomass, like wood, charcoal, waste and other manures for heat and power production.
  • Energy created by burning biomass creates greenhouse gas emissions, but at lower levels then burning fossil fuels like coal, oil or gas.

Cited from United Nations Climate Action
